Occupant Ejection

We have significant experience investigating, analyzing, and modeling occupant ejections from rollover crashes. We have published our models in peer-reviewed journals, and given scientific presentations on this topic. Our ability to know how and when an occupant is ejected in the roll sequence has been critical in several cases.

 

 

 

 

Loss of Control

The driver of the black coupe (bottom right inset photograph) was traveling around a curve on the inner lane when he lost control of his vehicle, crossed over the roadway centerline into oncoming traffic, and collided with the white sedan (top right inset photograph). We reconstructed the accident and demonstrated the driver of the black coupe was in excess of the legal speed limit at the time of the accident.

 

Industrial Accident

The driver of a tanker truck had just finished filling his trailer and was closing the top hatches via a bridge supported by a catwalk. The driver fell (down arrow) from the height of the catwalk to the ground. We used a laser scan to reconstruct the exact geometry of the rig, catwalk, and decedent's position of rest after the fall. This detail served as a reliable basis to reconstruct the accident and explain how the fall occurred.

 

Visibility

The driver of a vehicle, while stopped at the stop sign limit line, looking left, and anticipating a turn into the main thoroughfare, claimed he could not see the oncoming bicyclist, who had the right-of-way. A police photograph taken the day of the accident (left image, superimposed on our scene inspection photograph) illustrated the visibility obstruction created by a tree, vines, and other vegetation (red arrow, left image). After the day of the accident, but prior to our inspection, we found the land owners had cut down the tree and removed significant vegetation (red arrow, right image).
